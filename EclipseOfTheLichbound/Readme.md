# The Mad King's Nightmare
This Dungeons and Dragons 5th edition module is designed for level 6 characters and serves as a brutal test of your character build ideas in real encounter situations—no Dungeon Master required. Players must manage their resources, hit points, and loot carefully to survive the Mad King's twisted nightmares and rack up the highest score possible. So grab a friend or four, create some crazy multiclass (or not) characters, and prepare to face the terrors that await in the labyrinthine depths of the Mad King's Nightmare!

### Adventure Overview
Once a wise and benevolent ruler, the Mad King was driven to madness by his obsession with immortality. Desperate to escape the clutches of death, he delved into forbidden magics, eventually transforming himself into a Lich. This dark transformation came at a steep cost, severing his ties to humanity and binding his soul to a cursed phylactery. As his mind deteriorated, the Mad King lost all sense of reality, retreating into a labyrinth of nightmares where his twisted consciousness now reigns supreme. His kingdom crumbled, and his name was forgotten by the living, but his malevolent spirit continus to fester in the shadows.

In his unholy state, the Mad King has gained the ability to infiltrate the dreams of those who slumber, drawing their souls into his nightmarish domain. His influence over the dream realm is absolute, trapping adventurers in an endless cycle of torment and despair. As the characters sleep, they too are pulled into his nightmare realm, where the boundaries of reality blur. With each passing moment, the Mad King's grip tightens, turning their rest into a battlefield where survival is uncertain. Now, the characters must navigate this twisted labyrinth, confronting both the horrors within and the Mad King himself, in a desperate bid to escape his eternal nightmare. 



{{note
##### DM-Less Experience
Because this module is more of a board game than a standard D&D session, actions that would typically require a Dungeon Master's discretion, such as "Can I climb on the dragon's back?" are not possible. The focus is on strategy, resource management, and survival within the set rules.
}}

####  The Four Nightmares

The adventurers must navigate through four distinct nightmares, each uniquely crafted by the Mad King. These nightmares are populated with pre-generated undead and other nightmarish creatures, each governed by specific behaviors that dictate their strategies and behaviors.



{{pageNumber,auto}}
{{footnote PART 1 | OVERVIEW}}

\column


- **Resource Management:** Within each nightmare, players can explore to find artifacts or upgrade their equipment by tapping into the dark energies of the dreamscape. However, exploration comes with inherent risks, such as triggering environmental hazards or other mishaps.

- **Loot and Rewards:** After overcoming the challenges of each nightmare, players will roll on loot tables to acquire resources that can aid them in their quest. Strategic use of these items can turn the tide in subsequent encounters.


#### The Labyrinth Escape

Having survived the four nightmares, the adventurers must now navigate the intricate and ever-shifting corridors of the Mad King's Labyrinth. This is a theater-of-the-mind skill challenge where players can creatively utilize any of their skills to overcome obstacles, solve puzzles, and evade traps set by the lich.

Success in this challenge allows the party to confront the Mad King directly in a final battle. Failure, however, results in their souls being eternally bound within the labyrinth, lost to the Mad King's dark realm.

#### Final Confrontation with the Mad King

The showdown with the Mad King is designed to be a monumental challenge. The Mad King employs a variety of devastating spells, legendary resistances, and cunning tactics to thwart the adventurers. While victory is difficult to achieve, it is not impossible for those who have effectively prepared and utilized their resources.

- How quickly each nightmare is completed
- Completing nightmares without any character dropping to 0 hit points
- How well the party fares against The Mad King
- Bonus points for completely extra objectives

# Rules
In order to provide a seamless gaming experience not reliant on having a Dungeon Master behind the scenes, additional rules need to be defined. From character creation to recommened homebrew, this section covers the rules unique to this module, which supplement the normal Dungeon's and Dragons 5th Edition ruleset. Additionally, without a mediator, this module relies on official rules-as-written (RAW) and subsequent errata. 

### Character Creation
Players will start with the stat array: 17, 15, 14, 12, 10, 8, and can make any character they want following the standard rules for character creation found in the Player's Handbook (pg. 11). This module is designed to work with all races, classes, backgrounds, and other character options found in any of the officially published works. As with any module, any homebrew content you wish to use should be reviewed and approved by your table.

#### Starting Equipment
- Characters proficient in heavy armor may start with Splint mail, those proficient in medium armor may start with a Breastplate, and those proficient in light armor may begin with Studded leather.
- Any reasonable non-magical weapons and armor that characters wish to have can be included in their starting inventory (firearms are optional). 
- Spellcasters may have up to 200gp worth of material components for spells that require them, or they may choose to have the material components for any one spell, usable once.
- Characters that have a special bond with a mount or similar companion such as the Cavalier Fighter or Beastmaster Ranger, will be allowed to choose their mount or companion during character creation and have it present throughout the nightmare. 

### Monster Behaviors
Unless otherwise stated, each monster will act on initiave count of 13. Ties will be broken by whoever has the highest initiative bonus. If there are still any ties, players decide who will act first.

Each monster is assigned a set of behaviors that determine how it will act on its turn. The following guidelines apply to the actions of each monster unless a monster’s behavior states otherwise:








\column

- A monster will prioritize targeting the PC or summoned ally that is stated in the behavior unless in order to do so: 
  - Their movement would provoke an opportunity attack.
  - If a monster has been knocked prone it will stand at the beginning of its turn.
  - If a monster has been grappled it will only attempt to break the grapple if required to carry out its defined behavior.
-  If a monster can cast the shield spell it will do so only if it will take 15 or more damage from a single attack



### Increasing Difficulty
 Each nightmare becomes progressively more difficult to provide a varied and challenging experience. For each nightmare completed, all monsters except the Mad King and his minions have the following changes, stacking with each nightmare:

- This creature gains a +1 bonus to Armor Class, attack rolls, saving throws, and spell save DC
- The number this creature needs to roll a critical hit is reduced by 1
- When this creature rolls damage, it rolls an additional 1d6 damage and adds it to the total damage dealt
  
### Pre-Casting Spells
If a character has a spell, such as *Find Familiar*, that lasts an hour or more and could reasonably have been cast before they fell asleep, they can cast it before entering the nightmare without using a spell slot. This rule also applies to class features like the Artificer: Artillerist’s *Eldritch Cannon*. A general guideline is that if a spell or class feature is designed specifically for combat and can be cast in an action or less, it likely doesn't qualify. For example, Protection from Evil and Good would not be allowed to be cast prior to starting the first nightmare.

Once characters have cleared a nightmare of enemies, they have the remaining minute to cast any spells they wish in preparation for the next nightmare, before rolling to determine the next nightmare. 

### Resting
Due to the nature of this module, there is no time for short or long rests, as the entire adventure takes only around 10 minutes of game time to complete.

### House Rules
There are several house rules for the nightmares that are recommended, though they are not mandatory. Implementing all of them can enhance the experience, but you may also want to test a specific build with the house rules from your own campaign to see how it performs across different rule sets.


#### Flanking
This module uses the optional flanking rules from the Dungeon Master's Guide with a slight modification. When two creatures are attacking an enemy with melee weapons from opposite sides, the enemy is considered flanked. Any melee attack against a flanked creature is made with advantage.


#### Potions
Drinking a potion or adminisertering a potion to another character can be done as a bonus action. There are no penalties for having more than one type of potion in your system at the same time.

#### Assassinate
The following addition is made to the Assassin Rogue's *Assassinate* ability: If the target has not taken its turn yet this round, you not only attack with advantage, but also score a critical hit on a roll of 16 or higher.

#### Healing
To normalize healing in this module, any player that regains hit points always regains the maximum number of hit points possible from any healing.

#### Death Saving Throws
In this module, characters will not make death saving throws as normal. If a character is reduced to 0 hit points and does not regain any hit points by the end of the following round, the die and the Mad King claims their soul.


{{note
##### Consistency is key
Keep in mind, if you're aiming for optimal builds or teams, varying the house rules you implement can lead to inconsistent results. If you're focused on optimization, it's best to settle on a ruleset for your table and stick with it.

# How to Play

This section covers the basics of playing this module. Gameplay will take place over several 1-minute phases. Once you have created your characters and chosen your starting equipment, you are ready to begin. Read, or have someone at the table read the description aloud below to set the stage.

The following steps happen in each phase of play:
1) Roll a d12 and consult the nightmares table to determine which nightmare you will enter.

2) Read the nightmare's description, or select a player to read it aloud. These descriptions contain narrative backgrounds and special rules.
3) Roll initiative, and set the monter's intiative to 13 (unless otherwise stated). 
4) Fight the monsters using whatever resources you have until all are defeated, or until 10 rounds have passed, whichever happens first. If all characters die, you lose the game.

{{note
##### Alternate Objectives
In some nightmares, rather than defeating the monsters you will have alternative objectives such as escaping or completing some other task.

}}
5) Each player takes 1 action to loot the room. Roll on the loot table to determine what you receive. Items may be traded amongst the party.
6) Some rooms have things within them that characters can attempt to interact with in order to improve equipment or gain other benefits. Each character can only complete one interaction per nightmare, and characters may forego their attempt and take the help action to grant another character advantage on their attempt. Both scene interaction and taking the help action cost 1 action.
7) You will have until 10 rounds have passed since you entered the nightmare to prepare for the next nightmare. You can use this time to take actions, cast spells, or expend additional resources to give you an edge in the next nightmare.
8) Calculate the number of points earned for the nightmare add it to the point total, factoring in any bonus points earned.
9) At the end of 10 rounds, begin a new phase starting at step 1, applying the increased difficulty rules for each subsequent nightmare, until 4 nightmares have been completed. If any players have died, you may continue to progress, but you will continue to set up each nightmare based on how many players started.


### Escaping the Labyrinth 

After 4 nightmares, the characters are transported into the Mad King's labyrinth and must use their skills to navigate through it, avoiding the clutches of the Mad King himself.

In this phase, each turn unfolds in the following way:
- Roll a Strength (Athletics) check for the Mad King (1d20+5) to set the difficulty class (DC) of the escape
- Choose one character to contest the check. That character makes a skill check using any skill of their choosing. If they choose a skill that has already been used before by any character, the DC increases by 3 for each time it has been made before. They can use any skills, spells, items, or other modifiers as normal.
  - **Success:** On a success, continue to the next character, starting with step 1 to set the DC. On a roll of 20, this counts as 2 successes.
  - **Failure:** On a failure, the Mad King's strikes the character with his spectral blade, dealing 11 (3d6) slashing damage and (11) 3d6 psychic damage. On a roll of 1, the player takes twice as much damage. If this damage reduces the character to 0 hit points, they die and the Mad King claims their soul.
- After 6 successful skill checks are made, the players reach a portal at the end of the labyrinth.
- Characters will have one last round to prepare before entering the portal.

*Armor, Very Rare (requires attunement)*
:

open that door with the strength of the nearest animal. For example, if
there is a cow nearby, the user will have the "strength of a cow" while
opening this door.
